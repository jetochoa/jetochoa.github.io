A Balance

Getting complacent has beat the hell out of me, can’t lie.

Since leaving school, any opportunity for social interactions is eaten up by my mind. I’m spending just as much energy on developing my primary focus (music) as I am on course correction. “Course correction” is not allowing myself to be content staying in a set social environment (like a workplace). I feel almost forced into finding negatives with a situation so I’m able to obsess on the goal further.

So should I be excited to go to work? Yes and no. Going to work gives me money, which I use to fund projects, and I have the most social fun when I’m there. Why am I not excited? Because I should be farther in my career projection than I am. Being at work is a reminder that I’m still stuck in the system. There is no real freedom. The difficulty is that positive memories stick with me, and the happier I am the less I feel the hunger to succeed. Everything is just about keeping the hunger alive.

Happy enough to be healthily able to continue, but hungry enough to need (not want) to keep going.
